Platform for Enhancing Science Practical Education of Ordinary level students In Sri Lanka


Literature Survey

The literature survey for the proposed platform to enhance practical science education in secondary schools in Sri Lanka covers several essential areas. Firstly, studies on the integration of technology in education reveal the significant impact of personalized learning on student engagement and academic performance. Research indicates that features such as emotion detection, hand gesture recognition, and object identification have the potential to bridge gaps in traditional science education by simulating hands-on experiences.

Secondly, literature on adaptive learning systems underscores the importance of creating platforms that cater to individual learning needs. By monitoring student performance and emotional responses, adaptive systems can provide tailored content, improving learning outcomes and overall experience. This aligns with the platform's goal of using emotion detection to adjust learning pathways based on student feedback.

Thirdly, user engagement studies emphasize the role of interactive tools, such as hand gesture recognition and object identification, in enhancing student motivation and retention. The dynamic, hands-on nature of these tools can make science education more accessible and engaging for students, especially those in resource-limited environments.

Finally, privacy and security concerns in educational technology are a recurring theme in the literature. Ensuring data protection is crucial, especially when dealing with sensitive student information. Studies highlight the need for robust data encryption, secure user authentication, and compliance with data privacy regulations to ensure the safety and trustworthiness of educational platforms. Incorporating these security measures will be vital for the success and credibility of the proposed platform

Research Gap

The research gap identified in this study revolves around the lack of comprehensive, technology-driven solutions tailored specifically for practical science education in resource-constrained environments, such as secondary schools in Sri Lanka. While existing educational technologies offer tools like virtual labs and simulations, they do not fully address the unique needs of these students, especially in the absence of hands-on learning opportunities. Moreover, current platforms do not integrate advanced features such as emotion detection, hand gesture recognition, object identification, and chatbot support to personalize and enhance learning experiences. This gap highlights the need for a platform that not only provides interactive learning but also adapts to students' emotional and cognitive states, enabling more effective engagement with scientific concepts and improving academic outcomes. Additionally, there is a lack of research on the application and evaluation of these technologies in developing countries, where infrastructure limitations exacerbate the challenges in delivering quality science education. Addressing this gap through innovative, context-specific solutions could significantly improve science education in developing countries.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

The core problem addressed by this research is the significant gap in practical science education for secondary students due to insufficient resources in many schools, particularly in Sri Lanka. The lack of laboratory facilities, scientific equipment, and hands-on learning opportunities hampers students' ability to understand and apply scientific concepts. As a result, many students rely heavily on theoretical instruction, which leads to poor academic performance, disengagement from science, and reduced interest in pursuing science-related fields.To tackle this issue, the research proposes an innovative solution—a comprehensive online platform that integrates four advanced technologies: emotion detection, hand gesture recognition, object identification, and chatbot support. The platform aims to provide an interactive, personalized learning experience that adapts to individual students' needs, making practical science education more accessible. By offering features like emotion monitoring, which tailors content based on students' emotional states, and hand gesture recognition for quick responses in assessments, the platform seeks to engage students actively. Additionally, the object identification feature helps students visualize real-world scientific concepts, and the chatbot provides instant guidance.This solution addresses the longstanding problem of limited practical science education in resource-constrained environments, improving student performance and fostering greater interest in science.

Solution

Emotion Detection

Monitor students’ emotional responses as they interact with practical science questions. The system detects confusion or satisfaction and provides personalized content to support learning.

Hand Gesture Recognition

Enable students to answer multiple-choice questions using hand gestures, promoting quick thinking and engaging them in an interactive way to enhance memory retention.

Chatbot Support

Provide real-time assistance through an AI chatbot that answers questions on practical science topics and guides students through challenging concepts.

Object Identification

Allow students to upload images of science-related objects for identification, bridging the gap between theoretical concepts and real-world applications.

Interactive Learning Experience

Create a dynamic and accessible learning platform that adapts to students’ individual needs, enhancing engagement with science subjects and improving academic performance.

User-Friendly Interface

Prioritize user experience (UX) design principles in the development process. Conduct usability testing with teachers, and students to refine the interface and ensure intuitive navigation for all user groups

Objectives

1. Emotion Detection : Integrate an emotion detection system that analyzes students' emotional responses, such as confusion or satisfaction, to deliver personalized support and adjust content delivery based on their needs.

2. Hand Gesture Recognition : Implement hand gesture recognition to allow students to respond to multiple-choice questions using finger gestures, promoting quick thinking, interaction, and improved memory retention.

3. Object Identification : Develop a system where students can upload images of objects from the science curriculum, enabling the platform to identify and explain them, making theoretical knowledge more tangible.

4. Chatbot Support : Integrate a chatbot that offers real-time assistance and guidance on science-related queries, providing immediate answers and support to help students overcome learning challenges.

5. Privacy and Security : Ensure robust data privacy and security protocols within the platform, protecting user information and complying with relevant data protection regulations.

6. Interactive Learning Experience : Develop an interactive, user-friendly platform that tailors the learning experience to individual student needs, increasing engagement and academic performance in science subjects.

7. Continuous Improvement and Feedback Loop : Establish a feedback mechanism for students and teachers to provide input on their experiences, enabling continuous improvement and enhancement of the platform’s features.
